    Methodology

    1. Research Design

    This study adopts a comparative case study approach to assess the performance of ChatGPT in generating responses to exercises in Project Management and Business Process Management. The research design involves the creation of three types of prompts for each exercise, categorised as "Low," "Medium," and "High" based on the assumed level of knowledge required to formulate the prompt.

    2. Participants

    Participants in this study were experienced lecturers specializing in the subjects of Project Management and Process Management. Additionally, they served as the creators of the exercises and the overarching case study used in this research. The inclusion of experienced educators ensures a comprehensive understanding of the subject matter and the ability to critically evaluate the generated responses.

    Furthermore, the Tutor's solution, which acts as a primary reference for evaluating the generative AI responses, was collaboratively created and defined by multiple experts in the field. This collective expertise enhances the reliability and credibility of the reference solution, as it represents a consensus among seasoned professionals.

    The involvement of these experienced participants not only adds depth to the evaluation process but also ensures that the study benefits from a nuanced perspective that incorporates both academic rigor and practical industry knowledge.

    3. Experimental Procedure

    a. Prompt Generation:

    • Low: Prompts assume minimal to no knowledge and involve a straightforward reproduction of the exercise description.
    • Medium: Prompts assume a moderate level of knowledge, utilising either GenAI Prompting Know-How or Subject Knowhow.
    • High: Prompts assume advanced knowledge, employing both GenAI Prompting Know-How and Subject Knowhow with specific expectations outlined.

    b. Flagging:

    • Prompts are flagged according to the independent variable - Base know-how level (Low, Medium, High).

    c. Response Evaluation:

    • Responses generated by ChatGPT are evaluated based on accuracy and usefulness. Accuracy pertains to the correctness of information, while usefulness considers the relevance and depth of the response.

    4. Variables

    a. Independent Variable:

    • Base know-how level (Low, Medium, High). Prompts are flagged accordingly.

    b. Dependent Variables:

    • Accuracy Rating (1-10): Responses are rated on a scale from 1 to 10, comparing the accuracy of the generated content against the Tutor's solution.

      1. Factual Precision: The extent to which the response aligns with factual accuracy, ensuring that information provided is correct and free from inaccuracies.
      2. Alignment with Tutor's Solution: How closely the response mirrors the Tutor's solution, emphasizing the accuracy of content relative to the established reference.
      3. Adherence to Key Concepts: The accuracy of incorporating key terminologies and fundamental concepts relevant to the subject matter, showcasing a sound understanding.
      4. Awareness of Limitations: The acknowledgment and avoidance of overgeneralization or misrepresentation, showcasing an awareness of the limitations of the generative model.
      5. Precision in Details: The precision in presenting details and specifics, reflecting a high level of accuracy in the response.

    • Usefulness Rating (1-10): Responses are rated on a scale from 1 to 10, assessing the practical applicability and relevance of the generated content in comparison with the Tutor's solution.

      1. Relevance to Prompt: The extent to which the response is directly applicable to the requirements and context specified in the given prompt.
      2. Practical Applicability: How well the response can be practically applied in real-world scenarios, considering its usefulness beyond a theoretical context.
      3. Incorporation of Problem-Solving Elements: The effectiveness of the response in proposing solutions or addressing challenges presented in the prompt, showcasing practical problem-solving skills.
      4. Alignment with Best Practices: Adherence to industry or academic best practices, demonstrating an awareness of established standards and methodologies in the field.

    5. Data Analysis

    Quantitative analysis will be performed to identify patterns and trends in the generated responses across different know-how levels. Comparative analysis will be conducted to assess the effectiveness of ChatGPT in generating responses relative to the Tutor's solution.

    6. Limitations

    In acknowledging the scope and constraints of this study, it is essential to note that the evaluation of ChatGPT responses was conducted by a selected and small group of participants who were experienced lecturers and creators of the exercises. While this participant profile ensures a high level of expertise in the subject matter, it also introduces a limitation in terms of generalisability.

    One notable limitation is that the study did not encompass a diverse set of participants with varying knowledge levels. The evaluation was confined to a specific group of experienced educators, and as such, the findings may not fully capture the perspectives of individuals with different levels of familiarity with Project Management and Process Management concepts.

    Future research endeavors could benefit from incorporating a more diverse participant base, including individuals with varying levels of expertise, to provide a more comprehensive assessment of ChatGPT's performance across different knowledge domains.
